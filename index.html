Umair Ismail, PhD

This is a short intro to my professional career. Presently, I am a Postdoctoral Research Fellow at the University of British Columbia. My work, under the supervision of Dr Joshua Brinkerhoff, focuses on using scale-resolving simulations to understand the mixing of real gaeses in industrially-relevant environments. I am also involved in probing the role of pressure gradients and upstream, unsteady effects on the transition to turbulence of flow above airfols. This work makes use of direct numerical simulations.

Previosuly, I worked as a Research Assistant in the Aerospace Engineering Department at Iowa State University from 2014-2018. Prior to that, I did my MSc thesis in the Fluid Dynamics and Energy Systems Lab at TU Delft (2013-2014). During this period, I also spent some time as a Special Scientist in the Computational Sciences Lab, University of Cyprus (2013-2014).

Research Interests

My research to date has focused on using numerical experiments to investigate and model the flow-phyiscs of turbulent flows relevant to engineering regimes. More specifically, my interests and experiences include:

  • Use of computer simulations to investigate non-equilibrium effects in rough-wall flows
  • Reynolds-averaged modeling of complex flows using novel turbulence models
  • Algebraic structure based modeling
  • Blending of hydrogen in natural gas pipelines
  • Transition to turbulence
